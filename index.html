FAQ

A list of frequently asked questions to help you to give answer for your questions on your mind.

What is Fractional Investing?

Vuma Invest supports fractional investing for trading the U.S Stock market. This means you can buy parts of any stock based on how much you want to invest

How does the interest on my Vuma Invest wallet work?

Cash left uninvested in your Bamboo wallet will earn around 0.05% per annum in interest. It is an even better way to protect your money from inflation and devaluation because the interest rate on Bamboo is higher than the interest rates across Nigerian domiciliary accounts.

When does the US Stock exchange open and close?

The US stock exchanges open at 2:30 pm (Kenya time) and close at 9 pm (Kenya time) on weekdays.

How secure is my Vuma Invest account?

Making sure your account is safe is our biggest priority. We're committed to protecting your account with the highest standards of security available. We use state-of-the-art data encryption when handling your financial information and two-factor authentication (2FA) protection.
We're backed by top financial market operators and we not only meet traditional banking security standards, but we also exceed them. That's how much it matters to us.
The Securities Investor Protection Corporation insures your Vuma Invest account up to $500,000 and wallet balances on Vuma Invest are Federal Deposit Insurance Corporation (FDIC) insured.

How long can I hold my investments with Vuma Invest?

You can hold on to your US assets on Vuma Invest for as long as you want. You can sell off your stocks on Vuma Invest anytime you decide to.

Why Does Vuma Invest Need My BVN?

Vuma Invest is committed to upholding the highest standards of security. For regulatory purposes we must verify all users before opening their Vuma Invest accounts. We need your BVN to verify that you are who you say you are. Vuma Invest does not share your BVN with any third party entity nor do we use the information on your BVN for anything other than identity verification.
